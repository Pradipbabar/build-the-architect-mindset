# Phase 3: Architecture Transition

**Duration**: 12+ months
**Goal**: Start doing architecture work and transition to architect role
**Time Commitment**: 10 hours/week + applying at work
**Prerequisites**: Phases 1 & 2 MUST be complete

---

## Overview

You now have the skills. This phase is about applying them, building your reputation, and transitioning to an architect role.

**Key principle**: Start behaving like an architect BEFORE you have the title.

---

## Timeline & Progression

### Months 1-3: Observe & Document

**Focus**: Build architecture documentation muscle

#### Activities
- [ ] Document architecture of current system you work on
- [ ] Create architecture diagrams for your team's services
- [ ] Write ADRs for decisions your team makes (even if you're not making them)
- [ ] Ask "why" in every design discussion
- [ ] Study how senior engineers make decisions

#### Deliverables
- [ ] 3+ architecture diagrams of existing systems
- [ ] 5+ ADRs documenting team decisions
- [ ] Personal notes on decision-making patterns

#### Skills to Practice
- Diagramming
- Asking good questions
- Understanding existing systems
- Documenting architectural decisions

**Success Metrics**:
- Team finds your documentation useful
- You understand the "why" behind system design
- You can explain current architecture to new team members

---

### Months 4-6: Small Design Decisions

**Focus**: Own end-to-end design of small features

#### Activities
- [ ] Volunteer to design small features (not just implement)
- [ ] Write design docs for your proposals
- [ ] Present designs to your team
- [ ] Incorporate feedback and iterate
- [ ] Track outcomes of your decisions

#### Deliverables
- [ ] 3+ design docs for features you owned
- [ ] Presentations to team
- [ ] Implementation of at least 1 design

#### Skills to Practice
- Writing design docs
- Presenting technical proposals
- Handling feedback gracefully
- Making decisions with incomplete information
- Following through on implementations

**Success Metrics**:
- Your designs are approved and implemented
- Team trusts you to make design decisions
- You learn from at least 1 design mistake

---

### Months 7-9: Larger Scope

**Focus**: Lead technical discussions and mentor others

#### Activities
- [ ] Propose architecture improvements for existing systems
- [ ] Lead technical discussions (facilitate, don't dominate)
- [ ] Review others' designs and give constructive feedback
- [ ] Mentor junior engineers on design thinking
- [ ] Identify technical debt and propose solutions

#### Deliverables
- [ ] 2+ architecture improvement proposals
- [ ] 5+ design reviews provided to others
- [ ] Technical debt reduction plan
- [ ] Mentorship of at least 1 junior engineer

#### Skills to Practice
- Facilitating discussions
- Building consensus
- Giving constructive feedback
- Influencing without authority
- Balancing perfectionism with pragmatism

**Success Metrics**:
- Your improvement proposals are implemented
- Others seek your input on designs
- You've successfully mentored someone
- You're seen as technical leader on your team

---

### Months 10-12: Cross-Team Impact

**Focus**: Architect solutions spanning multiple teams

#### Activities
- [ ] Design solutions that span multiple teams
- [ ] Collaborate with other architects
- [ ] Present to senior leadership
- [ ] Build reputation beyond your immediate team
- [ ] Participate in architecture review boards

#### Deliverables
- [ ] 1+ cross-team architecture design
- [ ] Presentations to senior leadership
- [ ] Collaboration with architects outside your team
- [ ] Published blog posts or talks

#### Skills to Practice
- Working across team boundaries
- Presenting to executives
- Building coalitions
- Strategic thinking
- Long-term planning

**Success Metrics**:
- Successfully delivered cross-team solution
- Known as architect in your org (even without title)
- Invited to architecture discussions
- Ready to interview for architect roles

### Months 7-12: Prepare for External Opportunities

**Goal**: Ready to interview for architect roles

#### Resume & LinkedIn
- [ ] Update resume with architecture work
- [ ] Highlight designs you've led
- [ ] Include business impact of your work
- [ ] Update LinkedIn with architect keywords
- [ ] Get recommendations from senior engineers

#### Interview Preparation
- [ ] Practice system design interviews
- [ ] Prepare stories of architecture decisions
- [ ] Prepare to explain trade-offs
- [ ] Be ready to discuss failures and learnings
- [ ] Practice explaining technical concepts to non-technical audience

#### Portfolio Presentation
- [ ] Create portfolio website or GitHub repo
- [ ] Include 3-5 best designs (anonymized if needed)
- [ ] Include blog posts
- [ ] Include talks/presentations
- [ ] Make it easy for hiring managers to assess your skills

---

### Transition Options

#### Option 1: Internal Promotion
**Best if**: Your company values your work and has architect roles

- Discuss with manager
- Show portfolio of work
- Get support from senior engineers
- Formally apply for architect role

#### Option 2: External Job Search
**Best if**: No architect opportunities at current company

- Apply to architect roles
- Leverage your portfolio
- Network with other architects
- Consider contractor/consultant roles first

#### Option 3: Consultant/Freelance
**Best if**: You want independence and variety

- Build client base
- Start with small contracts
- Leverage your network
- Consider joining consulting firm first
