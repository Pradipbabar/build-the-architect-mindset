## Phase 3 Overview

Phase 3 is the TRANSITION phase. You now have the technical skills. This phase is about:

## Track C: On-the-Job Transition (Months 1-12)

### Progression Model (Follow in sequence)

#### Phase 3.1: Observe & Document (Months 1-3)
**Goal**: Build architectural documentation skills

**At work**:
- [ ] Document architecture of current systems
- [ ] Create architects diagrams for team's services
- [ ] Write ADRs for decisions (even if not yours)
- [ ] Ask "why" in every design meeting
- [ ] Study decision-making patterns of architects

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] 3+ architecture diagrams (C4 model)
- [ ] 5+ ADRs documented
- [ ] Personal notes on decision patterns

**Success metric**: Team finds your documentation useful

#### Phase 3.2: Small Design Ownership (Months 4-6)
**Goal**: Own end-to-end feature design

**At work**:
- [ ] Volunteer to design small features (not implement)
- [ ] Write design documents for your proposals
- [ ] Present designs to team
- [ ] Incorporate feedback and iterate
- [ ] Track outcomes of your decisions

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] 3+ design documents
- [ ] Presentations recorded
- [ ] 1+ designs fully implemented
- [ ] Lessons learned from each

**Success metric**: Team trusts your design decisions

#### Phase 3.3: Larger Scope Leadership (Months 7-9)
**Goal**: Lead technical discussions for larger components

**At work**:
- [ ] Propose architecture improvements
- [ ] Lead technical design meetings
- [ ] Review others' designs
- [ ] Mentor junior engineers on design
- [ ] Identify and propose technical debt fixes

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] 2+ architecture improvement proposals
- [ ] 5+ design reviews completed
- [ ] Technical debt roadmap created
- [ ] 1+ junior engineer mentored to completion

**Success metric**: Others seek your input on designs

#### Phase 3.4: Cross-Team Impact (Months 10-12)
**Goal**: Architect solutions spanning multiple teams

**At work**:
- [ ] Design solutions that span teams
- [ ] Collaborate with other architects
- [ ] Present to senior leadership
- [ ] Drive adoption of new patterns/technologies
- [ ] Participate in architecture board/council

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] 1+ cross-team architecture design
- [ ] Presentation to leadership (recorded)
- [ ] Architecture pattern adoption plan
- [ ] New initiative launched

**Success metric**: Known as architect in organization

## Track D: Portfolio & Public Visibility (Months 1-18)

### D1: Case Studies (5+ required)

**Types of case studies**:

**CS1: System I Designed** (1000+ words)
- Problem statement
- Constraints and requirements
- Solution architecture (diagrams)
- Trade-offs considered
- Lessons learned
- What would I change

**CS2: Architecture I Improved** (1000+ words)
- Original problems
- Analysis and root causes
- Proposed solutions
- Implementation approach
- Results and metrics
- Team impact

**CS3: Technical Decision** (1000+ words)
- Business context and problem
- Options evaluated
- Decision made and why
- Implementation and outcomes
- Unexpected challenges
- Lessons for future decisions

**CS4: Failure & Recovery** (1000+ words)
- What went wrong
- Root cause analysis
- How we fixed it
- Prevention strategies
- Team impact
- Personal learnings

**CS5: Innovation or Experiment** (1000+ words)
- Problem we were solving
- Hypothesis and approach
- Experiment setup and execution
- Results and learnings
- Business impact
- What's next

**Deliverable**: 5+ published case studies on blog or Medium

### Option 1: Promotion at Current Company
- Inform manager of Principal Engineer aspirations
- Request stretch projects
- Document your architect work over 6-12 months
- Make case for role transition

### Option 2: External Opportunity (Job Search)
- Use portfolio for interviews
- Leverage case studies and blog posts
- Reference mentors and recommenders
- Lead system design interview rounds

### Option 3: Hybrid (Internal + External)
- Build internal visibility
- Strengthen portfolio
- Keep options open with interviews
-  Negotiate based on market parity
