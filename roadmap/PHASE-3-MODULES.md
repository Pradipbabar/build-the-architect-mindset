## Phase 3 Overview

**Key Principle**: Start BEHAVING like an architect BEFORE you have the title.

Phase 3 is the TRANSITION phase. With Phase 1-2 complete, you have:
- ✅ Software engineering fundamentals (Go, databases, APIs, distributed systems)
- ✅ Design and architecture mastery (patterns, system design, resilience)
- ✅ Modern DevOps/Infrastructure skills (IaC, Kubernetes, CI/CD, Observability, SRE)

Now you need to:
- **Apply skills at work**: Be the architect in your organization
- **Build reputation**: Demonstrate expertise through documentation, decisions, and leadership
- **Develop leadership**: Influence teams, mentor others, guide technical strategy
- **Create portfolio**: Document your work and make it visible to the community

### Your Path: DevOps → Platform Architect → Principal Engineer

**Who you are becoming**: A Principal Engineer specializing in platform architecture, infrastructure, and developer experience. You understand how to design and operate systems at scale while enabling teams to build efficiently.

**Track Selection**: For your specialization, focus on:
- **A2: Platform Engineering** ← PRIMARY (explicitly building on Phase 2)
- **A3: Security Architecture** ← SECONDARY (complements platform work)  
- **A4: Performance Engineering** ← OPTIONAL (if applicable)

## Track C: On-the-Job Transition (Months 1-12)

### Progression Model for Platform Architects (Follow in sequence)

**Key at each stage**: Document everything, write ADRs, present your thinking

#### Phase 3.1: Observe & Document Infrastructure (Months 1-3)
**Goal**: Build architectural documentation skills and understand current state

**At work** (Platform Architect focus):
- [ ] Document platform architecture and current pain points
- [ ] Map developer workflows and toil areas
- [ ] Create diagrams for infrastructure (Kubernetes, CI/CD, IaC)
- [ ] Write ADRs for why infrastructure decisions were made
- [ ] Interview 5+ developers: "What slows you down?"
- [ ] Study how teams currently deploy and operate
- [ ] Document deployment procedures and failure modes
- [ ] Create infrastructure cost analysis

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] Complete infrastructure architecture documentation (current state)
- [ ] 5+ ADRs explaining infrastructure decisions
- [ ] Developer pain points analysis (survey with results)
- [ ] Deployment process documentation
- [ ] Infrastructure improvement opportunity list (prioritized by impact)
- [ ] Cost analysis (current spend and waste areas)

**Success metric**: Teams refer to your documentation; developers acknowledge your understanding of their experience

#### Phase 3.2: Platform Improvements Ownership (Months 4-6)
**Goal**: Own end-to-end platform improvement projects

**At work** (Platform Architect focus):
- [ ] Propose 2-3 infrastructure/platform improvements based on pain points
- [ ] Own design-to-delivery for one self-service capability
- [ ] Write platform design documents (target: developer readability)
- [ ] Present proposals to team and leadership
- [ ] Work with teams to implement improvements
- [ ] Measure impact (lead time reduction, deployment frequency, etc.)
- [ ] Gather user feedback on new capabilities

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] 2-3 platform/infrastructure design documents
- [ ] Presentations recorded (with demo of improvements)
- [ ] 1+ platform improvements implemented and live
- [ ] Metrics showing impact (lead time down X%, deployments/day up Y%)
- [ ] Developer feedback on new self-service capability
- [ ] Lessons learned documentation

**Success metric**: Teams request your input on platform decisions; developers praise new tooling

#### Phase 3.3: Platform Architecture Leadership (Months 7-9)
**Goal**: Lead platform-wide architectural initiatives

**At work** (Platform Architect focus):
- [ ] Propose platform-wide architecture improvements (e.g., multi-region, DR)
- [ ] Lead platform architecture working group or council
- [ ] Review infrastructure and deployment designs from all teams
- [ ] Mentor junior engineers on infrastructure and platform design
- [ ] Create platform roadmap (6-12 months)
- [ ] Identify and propose infrastructure tech debt fixes
- [ ] Drive adoption of platform standards and best practices

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] Platform architecture strategy document (6-12 month roadmap)
- [ ] 3+ platform-wide design proposals with trade-off analysis
- [ ] 10+ infrastructure design reviews completed with feedback
- [ ] Platform technical debt roadmap and remediation plan
- [ ] 2+ junior engineers mentored through design cycles
- [ ] Platform standards/best practices guide

**Success metric**: Teams proactively seek platform architecture input; leadership consults you on infrastructure strategy

#### Phase 3.4: Organization-Wide Platform Leadership (Months 10-12)
**Goal**: Platform architecture architect for the entire organization

**At work** (Platform Architect focus):
- [ ] Design platform solutions that span all teams and infrastructure
- [ ] Collaborate with security, product, and finance architects
- [ ] Present platform strategy to C-suite/board
- [ ] Drive adoption of platform standards across organization
- [ ] Lead major platform initiatives (multi-region, Kubernetes migration, etc.)
- [ ] Participate in architecture council as platform expert
- [ ] Build business case for platform investments (ROI on velocity, MTTR)

**Deliverables**:
- [ ] Organization-wide platform strategy and vision document
- [ ] 2+ major platform initiative designs (e.g., multi-region, K8s migration)
- [ ] Presentation to executive leadership (recorded) - business impact focus
- [ ] Platform adoption and change management plan
- [ ] Platform ROI analysis and business case
- [ ] 1-2 major platform initiatives successfully launched
- [ ] Platform engineering team roadmap

**Success metric**: Known as "the platform architect"; asked for input on strategic technology decisions; executives understand platform impact on business
