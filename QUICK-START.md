## Your Daily Workflow

### Every Day (30 min - 2 hours)
1. **Do the work**: Code, read, design (based on current phase)
2. **Take notes**: Document what you learned
3. **Track time**: Log hours honestly
4. **Update journal**: What did you do? What did you learn?

### Every Week (1 hour on Sunday)
1. **Review progress**: Check `README.md` and journal
2. **Update dashboard**: Check off completed items
3. **Weekly summary**: Fill out journal summary
4. **Plan next week**: Set goals for upcoming week
5. **Adjust if needed**: Too ambitious? Too easy?

### Every Month (2 hours)
1. **Deep review**: Are you on track?
2. **Calculate hours**: Did you average 10 hours/week?
3. **Assess gaps**: What's harder than expected?
4. **Adjust plan**: Make necessary changes
5. **Celebrate wins**: What did you accomplish?

## Common Questions

### "Can I go faster?"
Maybe, but don't sacrifice depth for speed. Better to take 5 years and be truly ready than rush through in 2 years with shallow knowledge.

### "Can I skip Phase 1? I already know some Go."
No. "Basic understanding of Go" is not enough. You need to MASTER it. Complete Phase 1 fully.

### "Can I learn multiple things in parallel?"
No. Sequential learning only. Finish Phase 1, then Phase 2, then Phase 3. No shortcuts.

### "What if I can't do 10 hours per week?"
Then adjust your timeline. 5 hours/week = 6-10 years. Be realistic about your constraints.

### "What if I get stuck?"
1. Debug for 30 minutes yourself first
2. Use documentation
3. Ask specific questions on forums
4. Talk to your mentor/accountability partner
5. Don't use AI as crutch

### "What if I want to quit?"
Read the "Emergency Reminder" section in `COMMITMENT.md`. Then decide.

### "Is this guaranteed to work?"
Nothing is guaranteed. But if you follow this plan with discipline for 3-5 years, you WILL have the skills. Getting the actual job depends on many factors, but you'll be qualified.
